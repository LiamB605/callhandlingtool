Advisor Call Handling Toolkit

Citizens Advice Cardiff & Vale | Consumer Service Specialist Guidance

1

Call Control & Interruptions

👩🏽‍💻
"That sounds like something I can help you with, I just need to ask a few questions to get the key facts..."
Scenario: Caller is over-explaining or irrelevant
  • "I am sorry to interrupt, but I want to make sure I am following the timeline correctly. Can we focus on when you first noticed the fault?"
  • "I have all the background information I need now. To move us forward, how exactly did you pay for the item?"
2

Hypothetical Questions ("What if?")

"But what if they say no? What if they only offer half?"
  • "At this stage, we have to see how the trader responds to your first step. Our next advice will depend entirely on their specific reply."
  • "The best way forward is to follow the steps we’ve discussed today. Once you have their response, if it is not what you were looking for, please call us back."
  • "I want to make sure the advice I give you is relevant. Let us focus on this first action, and we are here to support you."
3

Professional Boundaries

👩🏽‍💻
"We can provide you with advice on your consumer rights, but we are unable to get involved with the dispute itself or contact the trader on your behalf."
"What would YOU do?"
  • "As an impartial service, I cannot provide a personal opinion. By remaining impartial, we ensure the advice we give is based strictly on the law."
  • "I can explain the different legal paths available so you can choose the one that is best for you."
4

Managing High Emotion & Frustration

"I spent my last bit of money... I am completely stuck!"
  • "I can hear how frustrating this is. I am here now to help you find the best way forward regarding the consumer issue."
  • "I understand you are upset. Let’s focus on the facts so I can give you the right steps to take today."
  • "I realize this is not the news you hoped for, but I want to be honest about the legal position."
5

No Legal Rights to Argue

"Surely there is a law that says they have to?"
  • "I understand it feels unfair, but under consumer law, there is no automatic right to a refund just because [Change of mind / time limit]."
  • "I want to be honest with you so you don’t spend time pursuing a claim that doesn’t have a legal basis."
  • "While you don’t have a legal right, you could still ask for a gesture of goodwill, though they are not legally required to offer one."
6

The Last Resort: Court & Financial Loss

"I can’t afford court!"
  • "I hear what you are saying about costs. While court is the final legal step, it is entirely your decision whether you feel it is the right path for you."
  • "If you decide not to pursue court action, then unfortunately there are no further steps I can provide regarding this specific dispute."
7

Wrong Department / Signposting

"I’ve been trying to call my Local office all day!"
  • "I understand it’s frustrating. Our local offices are extremely busy. I can only advise that you keep trying their number."
  • "To ensure I give you the correct contact details for your area, may I take your postcode?"
8

Dealing with Abusive Language

👩🏽‍💻
"I genuinely want to help you, but I find it difficult when that language is used. Can we keep this professional?"
  • "I will have to end this call if the language continues, as I want to focus on solving your issue."
⚠️ Advisors can provide 1 - 2 warnings for abusive language. If the behavior continues, the Advisor can terminate the call, ensuring they explicitly tell the caller they are terminating the call.
9

Closing a Circular Call

Scenario: Caller repeats themselves after advice is given
  • "I’ve provided the full advice available today. To move this forward, the next step is for you to [Action]."
  • "I am going to let you go now so you can take those next steps we discussed."
  • "As we have reached the end of the advice I can offer, I will need to bring the call to a close now."
10

Final Closing

✅ MANDATORY: Provide the Case Reference Number and ensure they have written it down.
